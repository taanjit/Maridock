SECTION 3: SCOPE OF WORK / INSTRUCTIONS
Preparatory Measures:
  • Obtain necessary permits and safety clearances
  • Isolate system and implement LOTO (Lock Out Tag Out) procedures
  • Prepare work area with adequate lighting, ventilation, and access
  • Brief all personnel on safety requirements and work procedures
  • Ensure engine is in stop and locked condition for all measurements
  • Record vessel condition, trim, and draft for each measurement phase
Job Breakdown:
  1. Pre-Docking (Afloat) Measurements: Record crank web deflections for each unit at 90Â° intervals, ensure engine is stopped and locked, note vessel condition, trim, and draft
  2. During Docking (Keel Block Condition): Measure and compare crankshaft deflections with afloat readings, check for abnormal deflection variances due to hull distortion
  3. Post-Docking (Afloat After Undocking): Measure crankshaft deflection after engine settled on sea again, cross-check with pre-docking values for alignment validation
  4. Hot Condition Measurement: Run engine to normal operating temperature, record deflections to evaluate heat-related warping effects
  5. Documentation and Reporting: Submit full documentation including journal and crank throw numbers, vessel conditions, graphical plots and variation reports with all measured values and comparison graphs
Safety Controls:
  • All work to be performed under permit to work system
  • Use appropriate PPE: Safety helmets, safety shoes, coveralls, safety glasses
  • Ensure proper ventilation and gas-free certification if required
  • Implement fall protection measures where applicable
  • Engine must be completely stopped and locked before accessing crankcase
  • Ensure adequate lighting and safe access platforms
  • Hot work permits required if any grinding or welding operations needed
Standards & Compliance:
